Why It Matters

Better materials mean better performance. GLIDE sources only proven materials — no shortcuts, no substitutes.

Materials

What you ride matters. GLIDE selects materials based on performance data, not marketing claims. Every component starts with the right material — then we refine the shape, formula, or geometry around it.

Canadian Maple

All GLIDE wood decks start with 7-ply Canadian maple. This wood species offers the best strength-to-weight ratio for skateboarding. It flexes without breaking. It pops back after each landing. We source from managed forests and press each deck using calibrated pressure for consistent concave and camber.

Epoxy Resin

Standard decks use water-based glue. GLIDE uses epoxy resin. Epoxy bonds more completely between plies, creating a deck that stays stiffer longer and loses pop more slowly. The difference is noticeable after 50 sessions — epoxy decks still feel responsive while standard glue decks feel dead.

Composite Builds

For riders who want lighter, more resilient boards, GLIDE offers composite decks. These add fiberglass or carbon fiber layers between maple plies. The result is a deck that weighs less, resists razor tail longer, and maintains consistent flex over time. Composite decks cost more but last longer — a fair trade for serious riders.

Urethane Formula

GLIDE wheels use high-rebound urethane. Rebound measures how quickly material returns to its original shape after compression. High rebound means more energy return — you push less and roll longer. Our formula balances rebound with durability, so wheels do not flat spot easily.

Alloy Trucks

GLIDE trucks are forged from 356 aluminum alloy. This alloy offers the best combination of strength and weight reduction. Axles are heat-treated steel. Kingpins are grade 8 steel. No mystery metals. No corners cut.
