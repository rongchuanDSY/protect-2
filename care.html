How Long?

Deck: 2-6 months (depends on usage)
Wheels: 6-12 months
Trucks: Years with care

Care & Setup

A well-maintained board performs better and lasts longer. GLIDE components are durable, but they still need attention. This guide covers basic assembly, routine maintenance, and tips to extend the life of your setup.

Assembly Guide

Tools needed: Skate tool (or 9/16" socket + Allen key).
Steps: Attach grip tape to deck (or use pre-gripped). Bolt trucks to deck using hardware. Mount wheels onto axles with bearings inserted. Tighten axle nuts until wheels spin freely without side-to-side play. Adjust truck kingpins to preferred tightness — looser for carving, tighter for stability.

Routine Maintenance

Check axle nuts every few sessions — they loosen over time. Inspect decks for stress cracks or razor tail. Rotate wheels every month to wear evenly. Clean bearings when they feel slow or sound gritty. A few minutes of maintenance prevents mid-session failures.

Cleaning Bearings

Remove bearings from wheels. Pop off shields using a pin. Soak in citrus cleaner or bearing-specific solution for 10 minutes. Rinse with isopropyl alcohol. Let dry completely. Add one drop of light lubricant per bearing. Reinstall shields. Spin to distribute. Never use WD-40 — it attracts dirt.

Storage Advice

Store boards indoors away from moisture. Heat and humidity degrade wood and urethane over time. Avoid leaving boards in hot cars — glue can weaken, wheels can warp. Lean against a wall or hang on hooks. Do not stack heavy objects on top of decks.

When to Replace

Decks: Replace when tail chips significantly or pop feels dead. Wheels: Replace when flat spots affect rolling or diameter shrinks too much. Trucks: Replace if axles bend or kingpin breaks (rare). Bearings: Replace when cleaning no longer restores smooth spin.
