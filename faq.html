Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about GLIDE products, sizing, durability, and care. If you do not see your question here, reach out directly.

What size deck should I choose?

Deck width depends on your shoe size and skating style. Smaller riders or those focused on flip tricks often prefer 8.0" to 8.25". Larger riders or those skating transition often prefer 8.5" to 9.0". Try a few sizes if possible — small differences change how the board feels.

How long do GLIDE decks last?

Deck lifespan depends on usage. Casual riders get 6-12 months. Frequent street skaters get 2-4 months. Park skaters get 3-6 months. Our epoxy construction extends pop life compared to standard glue decks.

Are GLIDE completes good for beginners?

Yes. GLIDE completes are assembled with quality components that work together. Beginners benefit from reliable equipment that does not break or feel unpredictable. Our classic maple line offers affordable durability for entry-level riders.

How do I clean my bearings?

Remove bearings from wheels. Remove shields. Soak in citrus cleaner for 10 minutes. Rinse with isopropyl alcohol. Dry completely. Add one drop of light lubricant per bearing. Reinstall shields. Never use WD-40 — it attracts dirt and destroys bearings over time.

When should I replace my wheels?

Replace wheels when you notice flat spots affecting roll, when diameter shrinks noticeably (wheels wear down), or when urethane feels hard and slippery. Most riders replace wheels every 6-12 months depending on surface and skating frequency.

Do you offer warranty?

GLIDE offers a 30-day warranty against manufacturing defects. Normal wear — razor tail, chipped decks, flat spotted wheels — is not covered. Contact us with photos of the issue and proof of purchase for warranty claims.

Where do you ship?

GLIDE ships within North America. Standard shipping takes 5-7 business days. Expedited options available at checkout. Returns accepted within 30 days for unused products in original packaging.

What is the difference between street and park decks?

Street decks are narrower (8.0"-8.5") with steeper concave for flip tricks and technical control. Park decks are wider (8.5"-9.0") with mellow concave for stability on transitions and ramps. Choose based on where you skate most.
