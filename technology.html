R&D Process

Every GLIDE product goes through prototype testing, rider feedback, and material refinement before release. We break things so you do not have to.

Technology

Skateboarding technology is not about electronics. It is about materials, geometry, and the invisible details that change how a board feels. GLIDE invests in research because small improvements create real differences during a session.

Deck Construction

Our decks use 7-ply Canadian maple pressed with epoxy resin instead of standard glue. Epoxy is stronger and more consistent. The result is a deck that maintains its pop longer. We also offer composite builds with fiberglass layers for riders who want lighter, more resilient boards.

Wheel Formula

Wheel performance depends on urethane formula, not just durometer. GLIDE wheels use high-rebound urethane that returns energy rather than absorbing it. You feel the ground but not every crack. The formula balances grip for carving and slide for technical tricks.

Truck Geometry

Truck design affects turn response, grind clearance, and weight distribution. GLIDE trucks use a medium rake angle that provides predictable turning without feeling twitchy. The axle is heat-treated. The kingpin is recessed. Small details that add up.

Slight adjustments in body position change how a board behaves at higher speeds. Stiffness, flexibility, and material composition influence control under pressure. GLIDE focuses on those details. Its boards respond predictably even when conditions become demanding.
